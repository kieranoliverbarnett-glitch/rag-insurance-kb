# Policy Conversion

## What is a Policy Conversion?

A policy conversion is the process of changing a term life insurance policy to a permanent life insurance policy — such as whole life or universal life — without submitting to new medical underwriting. The ability to convert is a feature built into many term life policies, known as a conversion privilege or conversion option.

## Why Would a Policyholder Want to Convert?

There are several common reasons a policyholder might choose to convert:

- **Health changes:** If the insured's health has declined since the original policy was issued, they may no longer qualify for a new permanent policy at a standard rate — or at all. Conversion allows them to lock in permanent coverage without a new medical exam.
- **Changed financial goals:** The policyholder may have originally chosen term for affordability but now wants permanent coverage with a cash value component.
- **Estate planning needs:** Permanent coverage is often preferred for estate planning because the death benefit is guaranteed regardless of when death occurs.
- **Desire for cash value:** Term policies have no cash value. Converting to a whole or universal life policy begins the cash value accumulation process.

## How Does Conversion Work?

The conversion process typically involves:

1. Submitting a conversion request form to the insurance company.
2. Selecting the permanent product to convert to (options vary by carrier).
3. The new policy is issued at the insured's **attained age** — their current age at the time of conversion, not their original issue age.
4. Premiums for the new permanent policy will be higher than the term premium, reflecting both the permanent nature of the coverage and the insured's current age.

No medical exam or new health questions are required. The insured's original health classification is generally honored.

## Conversion Windows

Conversion privileges are not open-ended. Most term policies restrict conversion to a specific window, which may be defined by:

- **Time:** The conversion must be requested within a set number of years from the policy issue date (commonly the first 10 years of a 20- or 30-year term).
- **Age:** The conversion must occur before the insured reaches a specified age (commonly age 65 or 70).
- **Both:** Some policies use whichever limitation comes first.

It is important for policyholders to review their specific policy for conversion deadlines. Once the conversion window closes, the option is no longer available.

## Partial Conversion

Some policies allow for a **partial conversion**, in which only a portion of the term death benefit is converted to a permanent policy. The remaining term coverage stays in place for the remainder of the original term. This can be a useful strategy for balancing affordability with a desire for some permanent coverage.

## What Permanent Products Are Available for Conversion?

The available conversion products vary by carrier. Common options include:

- Whole life insurance
- Universal life insurance (traditional, indexed, or guaranteed)

The insurance company will typically provide a list of eligible products at the time of conversion request.

## Common Customer Questions

**Will my health history affect my conversion?**
No. The conversion privilege allows you to convert without evidence of insurability. Your original health rating is honored regardless of any changes in your health since the policy was issued.

**Will my premium stay the same after conversion?**
No. Permanent insurance premiums are higher than term premiums. The new premium will be based on the permanent product selected and your current age at the time of conversion.

**What if I miss the conversion deadline?**
Once the conversion window has closed, the option to convert without underwriting is no longer available. If permanent coverage is still desired, a new application with full underwriting would be required.

**Can I convert after my term policy has lapsed?**
No. The conversion privilege is only available on an active policy in good standing.
