# Whole Life Insurance

## What is Whole Life Insurance?

Whole life insurance is a type of permanent life insurance that provides coverage for the insured's entire lifetime, as long as premiums are paid. Unlike term life insurance, whole life policies do not expire after a set number of years.

Whole life insurance has two primary components: a death benefit paid to beneficiaries upon the insured's death, and a cash value component that grows over time on a tax-deferred basis.

## Key Features

**Lifetime coverage:** The policy remains in force for the insured's entire life, provided premiums are kept current.

**Level premiums:** Premiums are fixed at the time of issue and do not increase with age or changes in health.

**Guaranteed death benefit:** The death benefit amount is guaranteed and will be paid to the beneficiary regardless of when the insured passes away.

**Cash value accumulation:** A portion of each premium payment contributes to the policy's cash value, which grows at a guaranteed minimum rate set by the insurance company. Cash value growth is tax-deferred.

**Dividends (participating policies):** Some whole life policies are "participating," meaning they may pay dividends when the insurance company performs well. Dividends are not guaranteed but can be used to reduce premiums, purchase additional coverage, or accumulate as interest.

## Cash Value

The cash value component of a whole life policy grows slowly in the early years and accelerates over time. Policyholders can access cash value in two primary ways:

- **Policy loans:** The policyholder can borrow against the cash value. Loans do not require credit approval and are not taxable, but unpaid loan balances plus interest will reduce the death benefit.
- **Surrender:** The policyholder can surrender (cancel) the policy and receive the accumulated cash value, minus any applicable surrender charges. Surrendering the policy terminates coverage.

## Whole Life vs. Term Life

| Feature | Whole Life | Term Life |
|---|---|---|
| Coverage duration | Lifetime | Fixed term |
| Premiums | Higher, level | Lower, level during term |
| Cash value | Yes | No |
| Death benefit | Guaranteed | Paid only if death occurs during term |

## Who is Whole Life Insurance Best Suited For?

Whole life insurance may be a good fit for individuals who:

- Want permanent coverage that will not expire.
- Are interested in a policy with a guaranteed cash value component.
- Have long-term estate planning needs.
- Prefer the predictability of fixed premiums.

## Common Customer Questions

**Can I access my cash value at any time?**
Yes, through a policy loan or by surrendering the policy. Partial surrenders may also be available depending on the policy. Accessing cash value through loans does not trigger taxes, but surrendering a policy may result in a taxable gain if the cash value exceeds the total premiums paid.

**What happens to the cash value when I die?**
In most whole life policies, the insurance company retains the cash value and pays out the face amount (death benefit) to the beneficiary. Some policies offer riders that allow the beneficiary to receive both.

**What if I stop paying premiums?**
If premiums are not paid, the policy may lapse. Depending on the amount of accumulated cash value, the policy may automatically continue as a reduced paid-up policy or extended term insurance. These options are outlined in the policy's nonforfeiture provisions.
