# Term Life Insurance

## What is Term Life Insurance?

Term life insurance provides coverage for a specific period of time, commonly called the "term." If the insured person passes away during the term, the insurance company pays a death benefit to the designated beneficiary. If the insured outlives the term, the policy expires and no benefit is paid.

Term life is generally the most straightforward and affordable type of life insurance, making it a popular choice for individuals who want coverage during a defined period of financial responsibility.

## Common Term Lengths

- **10-year term:** Often chosen by individuals with shorter-term financial obligations or those closer to retirement.
- **20-year term:** One of the most common choices, suitable for covering a mortgage or raising children to adulthood.
- **30-year term:** Provides long-term protection, often selected by younger policyholders with long financial horizons.

Some carriers also offer 15- or 25-year terms depending on the product.

## Key Features

**Level premiums:** Most term policies feature level premiums, meaning the monthly or annual premium remains the same for the duration of the term.

**Fixed death benefit:** The death benefit amount is established at the time of application and does not change during the term.

**No cash value:** Unlike permanent life insurance products, term policies do not accumulate cash value. The policy has no savings or investment component.

**Conversion option:** Many term policies include a conversion privilege that allows the policyholder to convert to a permanent policy without undergoing new medical underwriting. Conversion windows are typically limited — often to the first 10 years of the policy or prior to a specific age.

## What Happens at the End of the Term?

When a term policy reaches its expiration date, coverage ends. The policyholder generally has a few options:

- Allow the policy to expire if coverage is no longer needed.
- Apply for a new policy, which will require a new application and may involve updated medical underwriting.
- Exercise a conversion option if available under the policy terms.
- Some policies offer a renewable option, though premiums typically increase significantly at renewal.

## Who is Term Life Insurance Best Suited For?

Term life insurance is well suited for individuals who:

- Want affordable coverage during years of peak financial responsibility (e.g., while paying a mortgage or raising children).
- Have a specific, time-limited need for coverage.
- Prefer simplicity and lower premiums over a policy with a cash value component.

## Common Customer Questions

**Can I cancel my term policy early?**
Yes. Policyholders can cancel at any time. Because term policies have no cash value, there is no surrender value returned upon cancellation.

**What happens if I miss a payment?**
Most policies include a grace period — typically 30 or 31 days — during which a late payment can be made without the policy lapsing. If payment is not received within the grace period, the policy may lapse.

**Can I increase my coverage amount?**
Generally, the death benefit cannot be changed after the policy is issued without applying for a new policy. Some policies may offer riders that provide additional flexibility.
