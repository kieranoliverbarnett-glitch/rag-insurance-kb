# Policy Loans

## What is a Policy Loan?

A policy loan is a loan taken by a policyholder against the accumulated cash value of a permanent life insurance policy — such as whole life or universal life. Because the loan is secured by the cash value of the policy, it does not require a credit check, income verification, or approval process in the traditional sense. The insurance company simply advances funds against the policy's cash value.

Policy loans are only available on permanent life insurance policies that have accumulated sufficient cash value. Term life insurance policies, which have no cash value, are not eligible.

## How Policy Loans Work

When a policyholder requests a loan, the insurance company lends an amount up to a specified percentage of the policy's cash value — typically up to 90% of the available cash value. The funds are not drawn from the cash value directly; instead, the cash value serves as collateral.

The loan accrues interest at a rate specified in the policy. Interest may be:

- **Fixed:** The rate remains constant for the life of the loan.
- **Variable:** The rate may adjust periodically based on an index or the insurance company's declared rate.

The policyholder is not required to make loan repayments on any schedule. However, unpaid interest that is not paid will be added to the outstanding loan balance.

## Impact on the Policy

An outstanding policy loan affects the policy in several important ways:

**Reduced death benefit:** If the policyholder dies with an outstanding loan balance, the death benefit paid to the beneficiary will be reduced by the loan balance plus any accrued, unpaid interest.

**Risk of lapse:** If the loan balance plus accrued interest grows to exceed the cash value of the policy, the policy may lapse. In this case, the outstanding loan amount may become taxable as income.

**Cash value remains credited:** Even though the policy's cash value is being used as collateral, the cash value generally continues to earn interest or dividends at its normal rate.

## Repaying a Policy Loan

Repayment of a policy loan is flexible — there is no required repayment schedule. Policyholders can:

- Repay the principal and interest at any time, in any amount.
- Allow the loan to remain outstanding indefinitely, as long as the cash value exceeds the loan balance.
- Repay only the interest to prevent the loan balance from growing.

It is generally advisable to repay loans to preserve the full death benefit and prevent the policy from lapsing due to an excessive loan balance.

## Policy Loans vs. Withdrawals (Partial Surrenders)

| Feature | Policy Loan | Withdrawal / Partial Surrender |
|---|---|---|
| Effect on cash value | No direct reduction; cash value serves as collateral | Directly reduces cash value |
| Tax treatment | Not taxable (loan proceeds are not income) | May be taxable if amount exceeds basis (total premiums paid) |
| Effect on death benefit | Reduces death benefit by outstanding balance | Permanently reduces death benefit |
| Repayment | Optional, flexible | Not repayable — permanent reduction |

## Tax Considerations

Policy loan proceeds are generally not subject to income tax because they are considered a loan, not a distribution. However, if the policy lapses or is surrendered while a loan is outstanding, the loan amount may become taxable to the extent it exceeds the policyholder's cost basis (total premiums paid).

## Common Customer Questions

**How do I request a policy loan?**
Most carriers allow policyholders to request a loan by phone, online portal, or written request. Processing typically takes a few business days.

**Is there a minimum loan amount?**
Yes, most carriers set a minimum loan amount, commonly $250 or $500.

**Will taking a loan affect my premiums?**
No, taking a policy loan does not change the required premium. However, it is important to continue paying premiums to keep the policy in good standing.

**What happens to my loan if I die?**
The outstanding loan balance and any accrued interest will be deducted from the death benefit before it is paid to the beneficiary.
