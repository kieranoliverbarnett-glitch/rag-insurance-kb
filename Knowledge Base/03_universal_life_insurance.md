# Universal Life Insurance

## What is Universal Life Insurance?

Universal life insurance (UL) is a type of permanent life insurance that offers more flexibility than whole life insurance. Like whole life, it provides lifetime coverage and includes a cash value component. Unlike whole life, universal life allows policyholders to adjust their premium payments and, within certain limits, their death benefit over time.

## Key Features

**Flexible premiums:** Policyholders can vary the amount and frequency of premium payments within limits set by the policy. As long as the cash value is sufficient to cover the policy's internal costs, the policy remains in force even if a premium payment is skipped.

**Adjustable death benefit:** The death benefit can often be increased (subject to underwriting) or decreased after the policy is issued.

**Cash value accumulation:** Premiums paid in excess of the policy's internal costs (cost of insurance, administrative fees) accumulate as cash value. Cash value earns interest at a rate set by the insurance company, subject to a guaranteed minimum floor.

**Transparency of costs:** Universal life policies are designed to show the policyholder how premiums are allocated — what portion covers the cost of insurance and what portion goes toward cash value.

## How Universal Life Differs from Whole Life

| Feature | Universal Life | Whole Life |
|---|---|---|
| Premium flexibility | Yes — can vary within limits | No — fixed premiums required |
| Death benefit flexibility | Yes — can adjust | No — fixed at issue |
| Cash value growth | Interest rate (variable, with minimum) | Guaranteed fixed rate |
| Complexity | Higher | Lower |

## Types of Universal Life Insurance

**Traditional Universal Life (UL):** Cash value earns interest based on current rates set by the insurer, subject to a guaranteed minimum. Premiums and death benefit are flexible.

**Indexed Universal Life (IUL):** Cash value growth is linked to a market index (such as the S&P 500), subject to a cap and a floor. The floor protects against market losses; the cap limits maximum gains.

**Variable Universal Life (VUL):** Cash value is invested in sub-accounts similar to mutual funds. Growth potential is higher, but cash value can decrease if investments perform poorly. VUL policies are securities products and involve investment risk.

**Guaranteed Universal Life (GUL):** Offers a guaranteed death benefit to a specified age (often 90, 95, 100, or 121) with minimal cash value accumulation. Provides permanent-like coverage at a lower cost than traditional whole life.

## Important Considerations

**Policy illustrations:** At the time of sale, policyholders receive an illustration showing projected cash value and death benefit under various interest rate assumptions. It is important to understand that non-guaranteed elements (such as interest crediting rates above the minimum) may not materialize as illustrated.

**Underfunding risk:** Because premiums are flexible, policyholders who consistently pay the minimum may find that the cash value is insufficient to cover internal policy costs over time, potentially causing the policy to lapse unexpectedly.

**Annual review:** It is advisable for policyholders to review their universal life policy periodically to ensure it remains on track based on current assumptions.

## Common Customer Questions

**Can I stop paying premiums entirely?**
If there is sufficient cash value to cover the policy's internal costs, the policy will remain in force without premium payments. However, if costs deplete the cash value completely, the policy will lapse.

**How is the interest rate determined?**
The insurance company sets the current interest crediting rate periodically. The policy guarantees a minimum rate (commonly 2% or higher), meaning the cash value will never earn less than that rate regardless of market conditions.

**Can I increase my death benefit later?**
In most cases, yes, but an increase will require evidence of insurability (medical underwriting). Decreases can typically be made without underwriting.
